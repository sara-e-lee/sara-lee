NEW POST-GRAD FELLOWSHIP PROJECT, IN COLLABORATION WITH THE HUNDREDS

October 14, 2021

The Yale School of Art is pleased to share the results of a new SoA post-grad fellowship project, developed in collaboration with the community-based streetwear brand, The Hundreds. Three students from our MFA class of 2021, each representing a different area of graduate study, were commissioned to create work specifically for the mass-produced medium, tote bag.

PAINTING/PRINTMAKING ALUM JORDAN CASTEEL '14 NAMED 2021 MACARTHUR FELLOW'

September 28, 2021

The Yale School of Art is pleased to announce that today, 2014 alumna of the Painting/Printmaking program Jordan Casteel, was named a 2021 MacArthur Fellow by the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation.

HAPPENING THIS WEEK

Sara Rudensky: Visiting Artist Lecture in Photography

  • Date: November 10, 2021
  • Time: 2:00PM - 3:30PM
  • Location: The Pool (Room G10), 1156 Chapel Street

Sarah Rudensky continues the Visitng Artist Lectures in Photography with a talk on Wednesday afternoon. Hosted by Jessica Tang, Photography MFA'22.

**Open to the School of Art community, although attendance outside of the Photo department is only available in a first-come/first-served basis until the capacity limitations of the space have been reached.

E-Artist Conversation: Rina Banerjee and Dean Kymberly Pinder

  • Date: November 11, 2021
  • Time: 12:30PM - 1:30PM
  • Virtual Event (Hosted by the Yale University Art Gallery)

Join artist Rina Banerjee, M.F.A. 1995, in conversation with Kymberly Pinder, Ph.D. 1995, the Stavros Niarchos Foundation Dean of the Yale School of Art. The conversation is moderated by Elisabeth Hodermarsky, the Sutphin Family Curator of Prints and Drawings, and opens with a welcome by Emily Bakemeier, Vice Provost of the University. Offered in conjunction with the exhibition On the Basis of Art: 150 Years of Women at Yale. Generously sponsored by the Martin A. Ryerson Lectureship Fund.

Closed captions will be available in English.

Meet with YQI Artist-In Residence DJ Spooky

  • Date: November 11, 2021
  • Time: 2:30PM - 3:30PM
  • Location: 36 Edgewood Ave., Room 204

MFA students and the broader SoA community are invited to meet informally with composer, multimedia artist, and writer Paul D. Miller, aka DJ Spooky — currently the artist in residence at the Yale Quantum Institute (YQI) in their collaboration with the Schwartzman center.

During this hour, Paul Miller aka DJ Spooky will be available to meet with interested students to talk about his practice. Also in attendance will be Florian Carle, PhD, Institute Manager at YQI, for students who might be interested in or have questions about the YQI artist in residence program.
